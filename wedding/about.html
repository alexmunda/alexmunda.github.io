Molly

Thinking back about how I met Molly, I am truly amazed at how we got to where we are today. Who knew that two high school kids that couldn’t even drive yet would end up getting married 10 years later. A lot has changed over the 10 years since I met Molly: we broke up after I graduated high school so I could be a ‘cool college kid’ (turns out that was a terrible idea), we started talking again because of a misunderstanding by one of her friends 3 years later (thank you whoever you are), we somehow managed to survive a long distance relationship for 2 years (never again), and we bought a dog (love you Moose). I can tell you one thing though, I would not be the person I am today if I did not have her in my life.

When she’s not kicking butt in nursing school or saving lives as a nurse intern, Molly spends her time being an all around amazing woman. She’s a fantastic chef and her baking is incredible. I couldn’t ask for a better Euchre partner though I’m surprised she's kept me around this long after the number of times I’ve trumped her ace. She told me that we needed a dog and I thought she was crazy, that turned out to be one of the best decisions I’ve ever made (second to asking Molly to marry me of course). She’s also the most thoughtful and caring person I know, one time she surprised me with coffee and beer just because I did well in a job interview. You can’t not marry someone after they surprise you with two of the best things on the planet.

Did I mention that Molly makes me a better person? Who knows where I would be today had we not gone on our second first date. Oh yeah, I know, I wouldn’t be madly in love with the woman of my dreams that I cannot wait to spend the rest of my life with. I also wouldn’t know that apples, cinnamon, and applesauce are three of the best things you can combine in your morning oatmeal. Oatmeal I could probably live without, Molly I could not. I love her more than I can express in words, and I always will.

Alex

I have to be honest when putting this website together I saved this section for last because I wasn’t quite sure on how to give Alex justice. I could provide a laundry list of attributes to describe him but the list might be endless. I would say there a few things that stick out to me on why I love Alex so much. First, he is a huge family man. Doesn’t matter if it is my family or his he loves to spend time with them. He is often the one asking if we are having family dinner on Sunday and what to bring. I love that Alex is so connected with both of our families and willing to stop what he is doing to help any of us out.

Secondly, Alex might be the most selfless person I know. I do not think in the ten years I have known him he has made a decision solely based on his wants. He is constantly checking in and seeing how he can help take a burden off of me. He gets up early each morning to take care of Moose and is always willing to help clean or cook. His actions help teach me each day to be a little less selfish. Alex has provided me with countless amounts of laughter and great memories. I do not know anyone with a better sense of humor or willingness to laugh at himself. Without Alex I would not have nearly as much joy and humor in my life. He has taught me not take the world so seriously.

Without Alex, I would be lost on how to make the perfect cup of coffee nor be the person I am today. My favorite characteristic about Alex is his ability to make me a better person. He is constantly pushing me to achieve greatness or do the best I can. He is patient and kind but not afraid to encourage me to be or to do better. He is an amazing partner that can often finish my sentences and says exactly what I am thinking. He has an incredible work ethic that is apparent in all that he does.
